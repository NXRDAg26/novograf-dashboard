Competition

Competitive intelligence for Novograf — who is competing, their weaknesses, and how to beat them in search and on LinkedIn

Eight direct competitors · UK signage and commercial interiors market · Keyword intelligence

Eight companies competing with Novograf for clients, search visibility, and LinkedIn audience in the UK commercial interiors, signage, and brand environments market. Each card identifies their specific weakness and the exact positioning Novograf should use.

Octink
Brentford, London · Part of Paragon Group / Service Graphics · Est. 1962 · 2,200+ followers
LinkedIn ↗ Primary rival
Now part of Paragon Group's Service Graphics division — a corporate acquisition that changed Octink from an independent operator to a division of a large group. Active in display graphics, hoardings, marketing suites, signage, exhibitions, and events. Strong in construction, property development, and live events sectors. 2,200 followers with content that is London-centric and product-showcase heavy — lots of project photos without the story behind them.
Their weakness
Corporate ownership shows in every post. Content is written by committee and filtered through brand guidelines. No employee ownership narrative, no Scottish or genuine national manufacturing story, no independence. Everything reads as marketing output from a large group rather than expertise from a team that cares.
Novograf's opportunity
The full-service, employee-owned, nationwide brand environment partner story beats Octink's corporate announcement style every time. Buyers who have worked with large corporate suppliers and experienced the gap between promise and delivery are exactly who Novograf's independence message lands with. Target Octink's SEO gap on "commercial graphics Scotland" and "brand environments national rollout" — terms they do not own.
Image Technique Ltd
UK · Commercial signage and branded environments · 28+ years · In-house manufacture and install
LinkedIn ↗ Signage specialist
Specialist commercial signage company with 28 years of experience and full in-house design through manufacture through installation. Clients include Halfords, Odeon, M&S, shopping centres, and leisure attractions. Regular LinkedIn content but uses emojis heavily — enthusiastic but not authoritative. Strong on wayfinding, built-up letters, illuminated signage, and digital screens.
Their weakness
Content reads as project announcements rather than industry insight. No thought leadership, no client story depth, no sustainability narrative. Positioned as a signage company rather than a brand environment partner — a narrower proposition that limits who they can win.
Novograf's opportunity
The breadth of Novograf's surface solutions (floor graphics, wall graphics, surface emulation, replicas, Eikonic panels) is significantly wider than Image Technique's signage focus. Own the brand environment conversation, not just the signage conversation. Target their keyword gap on surface emulation, wall graphics, and floor graphics.
XG Group (Xtreme Graphics)
Brackley, Northants · Large format print production · Est. 2001 · Exhibitions, events, retail, architecture
LinkedIn ↗ Print house
Large format digital print production house with in-house dye sublimation, direct-to-fabric, and UV wide format printing up to 5m. Founded 2001, working across exhibitions, live events, architecture, construction, retail, and commercial interiors. Primarily a production supplier rather than a brand environment partner. LinkedIn output is modest and technical — focused on capability and specs.
Their weakness
Production-led positioning means they compete on capability and price rather than outcome and brand transformation. They print things — they do not manage brand environments. No installation, no project management, no national rollout capability.
Novograf's opportunity
A brand environment project is never just a print job. Installation, project management, quality control across multiple sites, and ongoing brand compliance are what XG Group cannot offer. Lead with outcome, not equipment. Target keyword gaps around "brand environment installation UK" and "retail graphics project management".
Vinyline Signs and Graphics
North West England · Signage specialists · 40+ years · Retail and supermarket focus
LinkedIn ↗ Regional signage
North West signage specialists with over 40 years in business. Strong focus on supermarket and retail exterior signage, vehicle wraps, and shop front signage. Work with supermarket brands on consistent branded exterior programmes. Regional in operational footprint with low-volume LinkedIn presence and product-led content.
Their weakness
Strong regional presence but limited national rollout capability and no surface emulation or full brand environment story. Cannot deliver what Co-op, Greggs, or Sainsbury's need at national scale. No sustainability narrative, no employee ownership, no Scottish manufacturing heritage.
Novograf's opportunity
National brand managers working with Vinyline on regional projects need a partner with UK-wide rollout capability. Novograf's track record with Co-op, Sainsbury's, and Greggs on national programmes is the proof. Target keyword gap on "national retail signage rollout" and "supermarket brand environment UK".
Harvey Fit Out Group
UK · £53m turnover · Top 20 UK fit-out contractor · Retail, leisure, hospitality · 40+ years
LinkedIn ↗ Structural contractor
Major national fit-out contractor with £53 million turnover, 40+ years in business, consistently ranked among the UK's top 20 fit-out contractors. Delivers full structural fit-outs across retail, leisure, and hospitality. Professional LinkedIn presence with project-focused, corporate content. Messaging is contractor-led rather than brand-led.
Their weakness
Harvey does the structural fit-out — Novograf does the surface, graphics, and brand layer that makes the fit-out feel finished and on-brand. These are complementary not competing services in most cases. Harvey needs a graphics and surface partner for their projects.
Novograf's opportunity
Be in Harvey's supply chain, not just competing against them. Position as the graphics and surface partner for Harvey fit-out programmes. The same brands they work with are Novograf clients. Being visible on LinkedIn in their space is the first step to being in their supply chain.
KDM Group
Dalgety Bay, Fife, Scotland · Turnkey fit-out and joinery · Est. 1998 · Tesco, Aldi, McDonald's, Amazon
LinkedIn ↗ Fellow Scottish company
Specialist interior fit-out and joinery contractor based in Dalgety Bay, Fife — a fellow Scottish company. Works with Tesco, Aldi, McDonald's, Amazon, and Primark on national programmes. Offices across UK and Europe. Their work is structural and joinery-led — doors, washrooms, ceilings, partitions, fixtures. LinkedIn content is professional, project-heavy, and community-focused.
Their weakness
KDM does structure and joinery — they do not deliver the graphics, surface emulation, or visual brand layer. There is a genuine gap that is a genuine opportunity for Novograf to be their surface and graphics partner on Scottish and national fit-out programmes.
Novograf's opportunity
Be the Scottish-based graphics and surface partner that completes KDM's programmes. Position as complementary capability — two Scottish businesses delivering the complete fit-out together. Content celebrating Scottish manufacturing capability in commercial interiors positions both companies and resonates with Scottish brands.
EE Smith Contracts
Leicester · Prestige hotel and luxury interior fit-out · Est. 1897 · Peninsula, Lanesborough, Mandarin Oriental
LinkedIn ↗ Ultra-luxury end
One of the UK's most prestigious interior fit-out contractors, established 1897. Specialists in prestige hotels, luxury residences, and high-end commercial interiors. Clients include The Peninsula London, The Lanesborough, and Mandarin Oriental. Beautifully produced LinkedIn content with high-quality photography and aspirational tone. They operate at the ultra-luxury single-site end of the market.
Their weakness
They operate at the ultra-luxury end — the opposite end of the scale from Novograf's national retail and hospitality rollout capability. EE Smith has never competed for the work Novograf wins and never will. They are not a competitor in practice — they are a different market entirely.
Novograf's opportunity
Own the high-volume, brand-consistent, national rollout positioning that EE Smith never competes for. Position as the UK's leading brand environment delivery partner for national retail and hospitality chains — a completely different value proposition that serves a different and much larger client base.
Pacy and Wheatley Interiors
Doncaster · Shopfitting and interior fit-out · Est. 1968 · Tesco, Holiday Inn, Marriott, Aldi, Magnet
LinkedIn ↗ Shopfitting contractor
Over 50 years of shopfitting and interior fit-out experience. Works with Tesco's convenience store rollout programme, Holiday Inn, Marriott, Aldi, and Magnet on national programmes. Covers retail, healthcare, hospitality, education, and commercial sectors. Strong on multi-site consistency and project management. Modest LinkedIn presence, project-focused.
Their weakness
Pacy and Wheatley deliver the structural shopfit — not the visual brand layer. Graphics, surface emulation, wayfinding, and printed environments are not in their scope. The same brands they work with are Novograf clients — Tesco, Holiday Inn, Marriott. There is a direct supply chain opportunity.
Novograf's opportunity
Be the brand environment subcontractor on Pacy and Wheatley programmes. Position as the ideal graphics and surface partner for multi-site shopfitting programmes — consistent quality, predictable delivery, national installation capability, ISO 9001 accredited. Being visible on LinkedIn in their space is the first step.
