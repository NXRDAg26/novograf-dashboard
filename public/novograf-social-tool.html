LinkedIn strategy signals — what matters for Novograf in 2026
Project photography is the single biggest lever
Novograf's work is inherently visual. Every completed project is a content asset. Before and after shots, wide environment photography, and detail close-ups of surface finishes consistently outperform all other content types in B2B commercial interiors. The challenge is systematically capturing and publishing this work rather than leaving it on a hard drive.
Employee ownership is an untapped story
Novograf is one of the UK's most notable employee-owned businesses in manufacturing. This is a powerful, differentiated story that almost no competitor can tell. In 2026, authenticity and ownership culture are among the highest-engagement LinkedIn topics. Novograf is sitting on a content goldmine it has barely touched.
Named clients are the proof that opens doors
Co-op, Greggs, Sainsbury's, NatWest, M&S, Travelodge — these names carry enormous credibility with any retail or hospitality brand manager scrolling LinkedIn. Every piece of content that references a named client is doing prospecting work. The audience thinks: if they work for Greggs, they can work for us.
Sectors, not just services
Retail, hospitality, healthcare, rail, banking — Novograf operates across multiple sectors. LinkedIn content that speaks to a specific sector (retail refurb season, hotel rebranding, bank branch refresh) performs significantly better than generic graphics and signage posts because it lands directly in a relevant buyer's feed at the right moment.

Short video program — 8-week brief for videographer

A structured production program built around Novograf's core content themes. Each video is designed for LinkedIn native upload, 60 to 90 seconds maximum. Shot list and spoken brief included for each video. All can be filmed on site with a smartphone plus a lapel mic.

Week 1 — What we do: site visit walkthrough 60 sec

Objective: introduce Novograf to a cold audience by showing the work, not describing it. No pitch. Pure process.

1
Opening — exterior of client facility
Camera: wide establishing shot of industrial site. No branding on site needed. Voiceover: "This is what a commercial interiors audit looks like in practice."
2
Shot — engineer connecting test equipment
Camera: close-up of equipment connection at pipe junction. Voiceover: "We start by connecting our particle count and moisture analyser directly to the distribution line."
3
Shot — reading the display
Camera: close-up of equipment readout or tablet display. Voiceover: "Within minutes we can tell you if your air meets ISO 8573 purity standards — or where it is failing."
4
Shot — engineer to camera
Camera: medium shot, direct to camera on site. Line: "Most clients are surprised by what we find. Commercial interiors problems rarely announce themselves — until they become expensive ones."
5
Caption for post
We spent the morning at a client site in northern UK. This is what 35 years of commercial interiors expertise looks like in practice. [Link in comments for a free air quality assessment.]
Week 2 — The cost of doing nothing: energy waste explained 90 sec

Objective: quantify the commercial pain of inaction. Hook the plant manager or financial director who controls the budget.

1
Hook — direct to camera, office setting
Line: "In most manufacturing plants, commercial interiors is the fourth biggest energy cost. Most finance directors have never looked at it." Hold 2 seconds. Cut.
2
Visual — diagram or whiteboard animation
Simple hand-drawn or filmed whiteboard: circle labelled "air system" with arrows to energy cost, productivity, and equipment life. Voiceover narrates each arrow briefly.
3
Stat card — on screen text
Text overlay: "A single 3mm leak in a commercial interiors line can cost over 1,500 euros per year." Hold for 3 seconds. Voiceover: "And most plants have more than one."
4
Close — engineer direct to camera
Line: "An audit pays for itself. We have seen clients recover the cost within three months of our first visit." Soft smile. Cut.
Week 3 — Client result: before and after 60 sec

Objective: social proof without a named client. Anonymised story format. Numbers are the hero.

1
Setup — narrator on camera
Line: "Last quarter we worked with a food manufacturing client in central UK. Their commercial interiors system looked fine on paper. It was not."
2
Finding 1 — text card or cut to site
Card or voiceover over site footage: "We found 7 leaks across the distribution network. Estimated cost: over 9,000 euros per year."
3
Finding 2 — continuation
Card: "Moisture contamination in the line was causing intermittent equipment faults. The client had been paying for repairs, not fixing the root cause."
4
Resolution — narrator
Line: "We fixed the system in two visits. The client saved more in the first year than they spent on five years of reactive maintenance. That is what we do."
Week 4 — Employee ownership: the story behind the company 75 sec

Objective: humanise the Novograf brand with the employee ownership story. This is a differentiator no competitor can match.

1
Hook
Line: "In 2015, the founders of Novograf turned down a profitable sale to a large American company. They chose their staff instead. Here is why that matters to every client we work with."
2
Team footage — workshop or production floor
Camera: panning shot of team at work in the East Kilbride facility. Voiceover: "Every person you work with at Novograf owns a share of this business."
3
Direct to camera
Line: "When you own the company, you go the extra mile. Not because you are told to. Because it is yours."
4
Caption
We have been employee-owned since 2015. Every project we work on, every installation we complete — our team does it as owners. That is the Novograf difference. #EmployeeOwned #Scotland #Novograf
