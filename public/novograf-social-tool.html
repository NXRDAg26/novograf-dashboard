LinkedIn strategy signals — what matters for Novograf in 2026
Project photography is the single biggest lever
Novograf's work is inherently visual. Every completed project is a content asset. Before and after shots, wide environment photography, and detail close-ups of surface finishes consistently outperform all other content types in B2B commercial interiors. The challenge is systematically capturing and publishing this work rather than leaving it on a hard drive.
Employee ownership is an untapped story
Novograf is one of the UK's most notable employee-owned businesses in manufacturing. This is a powerful, differentiated story that almost no competitor can tell. In 2026, authenticity and ownership culture are among the highest-engagement LinkedIn topics. Novograf is sitting on a content goldmine it has barely touched.
Named clients are the proof that opens doors
Co-op, Greggs, Sainsbury's, NatWest, M&S, Travelodge — these names carry enormous credibility with any retail or hospitality brand manager scrolling LinkedIn. Sectors, not just services
Retail, hospitality, healthcare, rail, banking — Novograf operates across multiple sectors. LinkedIn content that speaks to a specific sector (retail refurb season, hotel rebranding, bank branch refresh) performs significantly better than generic graphics and signage posts because it lands directly in a relevant buyer's feed at the right moment.

Short video program — 8-week brief for videographer

A structured production program built around Novograf's core content themes. Each video is designed for LinkedIn native upload, 60 to 90 seconds maximum. Shot list and spoken brief included for each video. All can be filmed on site with a smartphone plus a lapel mic.

Week 1 — What we do: project walkthrough 60 sec

Objective: introduce Novograf to a cold audience by showing the work, not describing it. No pitch. Pure transformation.

1
Opening — before shot
Camera: wide shot of blank or tired retail or hospitality space before installation. Voiceover: "This is what we walked into."
2
Installation footage
Camera: team applying wall graphics, surface wraps, or floor graphics. Fast cuts. No narration — let the activity speak.
3
Reveal — after shot
Camera: same wide shot, now showing the completed branded environment. Hold for 3 seconds. Voiceover: "This is what we left behind."
4
Direct to camera
Line: "Novograf. Brand environments from East Kilbride, delivered nationwide." Caption: Design, manufacture, install — all Novograf. #CommercialInteriors #BrandEnvironments #Novograf
Week 2 — Surface emulation: not what you think it is 75 sec

Objective: educate the audience on what surface emulation actually is and the commercial opportunity it represents for retail and hospitality brands.

1
Hook — direct to camera
Line: "What if you could make a wall look like exposed brick, aged timber, or brushed steel — without any structural work and without closing for a week?" Hold 2 seconds. Cut.
2
Reveal — surface examples
Camera: slow pan across different surface emulation finishes — brick, wood, tile, marble, metal. No narration. Let the visuals work.
3
Commercial point — direct to camera
Line: "A full structural refit of a retail unit can cost tens of thousands and take weeks. Surface emulation delivers the same visual result for a fraction of the cost, with the space trading throughout."
4
Close
Line: "This is what Novograf does. Brand environments at retail and hospitality scale, from East Kilbride." Caption: The same wall. A completely different space. #SurfaceEmulation #RetailDesign #Novograf
Week 3 — Client result: national rollout in numbers 60 sec

Objective: social proof with scale. Show what a national rollout programme actually looks like in practice. Numbers are the hero.

1
Setup
Line: "Earlier this year we delivered a brand refresh programme for a major UK retail client. Here is what that looked like." No client name needed — the scale speaks for itself.
2
Stat cards — on screen text
Card 1: "[X] sites across the UK." Card 2: "All completed within [X] weeks." Card 3: "Zero trading downtime reported." Voiceover reads each card in turn.
3
Installation montage
Fast cuts from multiple site installations — different locations, same brand consistency. Show the scale through variety.
4
Close — direct to camera
Line: "That is what national rollout capability means. Same standard, every site, every time." Caption: Consistent brand environments at national scale. That is Novograf. #BrandRollout #RetailFitOut #Novograf
Week 4 — Employee ownership: the story behind the company 75 sec

Objective: humanise the Novograf brand with the employee ownership story. This is a differentiator no competitor can match.

1
Hook
Line: "In 2015, the founders of Novograf turned down a profitable sale to a large American company. They chose their staff instead. Here is why that matters to every client we work with."
2
Team footage — workshop or production floor
Camera: panning shot of team at work in the East Kilbride facility. Voiceover: "Every person you work with at Novograf owns a share of this business."
3
Direct to camera
Line: "When you own the company, you go the extra mile. Not because you are told to. Because it is yours."
4
Caption
We have been employee-owned since 2015. Every project we work on, every installation we complete — our team does it as owners. That is the Novograf difference. #EmployeeOwned #Scotland #Novograf

Instagram content pillars
Pillar 1 — Project photography
Before and after shots, wide environment photography, surface close-ups, and installation detail. This is the core of Novograf's Instagram. Every completed project is a potential post. Static carousels of a single project (5-8 images) are the highest-save format — designers and brand managers save them for reference. Caption should name the environment type (retail, hospitality, workplace), the surface solution used, and leave space for the visual to do the work.
Pillar 2 — Reels and timelapse
Instagram Reels of installation timelapses are the highest-reach content type on the platform for commercial interiors companies. A 15 to 30-second timelapse of an installation — blank walls to branded environment — consistently gets 3 to 5 times organic reach compared to static posts. No narration needed. Good music choice. Brand watermark in corner. Cross-post to LinkedIn as native video. Aim for 2 Reels per week minimum.
Pillar 3 — Surface and material detail
Macro and close-up photography of surface textures, finishes, Eikonic panels, replica materials, and print quality. This content performs exceptionally well with interior designers, specifiers, and brand managers who are evaluating suppliers. A carousel showing 6 to 8 different surface emulation finishes side by side is highly saved and shared. Helps followers understand the quality and breadth of what is possible.
Pillar 4 — Behind the scenes and team
The employee ownership story works on Instagram too — but in a visual, human way. Production floor footage, team on site, the East Kilbride facility, Eikonic showroom, packing and despatch. This content builds trust and humanises the brand. It also performs well for employer brand — Novograf as a place to work. 1 post per week from behind the scenes keeps the human element visible without overwhelming the project content.

Reels (timelapse, reveals) — post to Instagram first, then upload natively to LinkedIn as a video post same day. What stays Instagram-only
Behind the scenes stories, Eikonic material swatches, texture close-ups, and team/personal content is better suited to Instagram's visual-first audience.
